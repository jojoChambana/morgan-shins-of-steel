Normal, IL Woman Receives Certificate Confirming What Everyone Already Knew

Town Of Normal Officials Praise Schulte's "Extraordinary Lower-Leg Commitment" In Ceremony Lasting Slightly Longer Than Anyone Planned

By Staff Reporter • The Bunion • March 12, 2026

NORMAL, IL — In what municipal officials are calling "an important moment for the town and also for shins," the Town of Normal formally recognized local resident Morgan Schulte Tuesday for possessing the longest shins in the community, concluding a months-long measurement initiative that sources confirm was originally just a real estate course completion certificate someone had lying around.

"Morgan has always had exceptional shins," said Town Director Megan Young, who signed the certificate alongside Manager Shenyari Simmons in a ceremony held in a conference room that smelled like a previous meeting. "The Town of Normal felt it was time to say that out loud, officially, in cursive, with a seal."

Schulte, who reportedly received the framed document with an expression that witnesses described as "difficult to read," confirmed she had indeed completed a real estate course, a fact the certificate also mentions, apparently unbothered by the contradiction.

"We had already printed it."
— Unnamed Town of Normal staffer, on why the line reading "for completing the real estate course" was not removed from the shin certificate

The certificate, which features the Town of Normal's official embossed seal and the signatures of two municipal employees who have not publicly commented on how their afternoon went, lists Schulte's achievement above a line reading "for completing the real estate course," which officials say was not removed because, in the words of one staffer, "we had already printed it."

The measurement process, described by sources as "rigorous," reportedly involved standard tape measures, two yardsticks deployed sequentially, a step ladder borrowed from facilities management, and one intern who was later reassigned after whispering "that can't be right" during the final verification.

Town Manager Simmons, reached by phone Thursday, stated that the certificate template had to be "extended twice" to accommodate the full scope of Schulte's achievement, declining to specify whether this referred to the document's physical dimensions or the emotional weight of the occasion.

Community response has been largely supportive, with several residents noting they had long suspected Schulte's shins were exceptional but lacked the institutional framework to say so publicly.

"Her shins arrive about thirty seconds before the rest of her," said one coworker, who asked not to be named. "We've started scheduling meetings accordingly."

The Town of Normal has not announced plans to retire the title, though officials confirmed no challenger has yet come forward. A proposed annual celebration, tentatively titled ShinFest, is under review by a subcommittee that was formed, sources say, primarily to give the intern something to do.

At press time, Schulte had been seen leaving the building in what onlookers described as the longest pair of jeans anyone had personally witnessed, the lower six inches of which had reportedly already reached the parking lot.
